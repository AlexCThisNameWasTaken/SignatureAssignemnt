Blue Jay - Cyanocitta cristata

Blue jays are recognizable birds known for their striking blue plumage, prominent crest, and loud calls. They are part of the crow family, are intelligent and adaptable, and have a reputation for being aggressive and territorial. Blue jays are omnivorous, feeding on a variety of foods including insects, nuts, seeds, and even eggs and nestlings. They are common in eastern North America, especially in deciduous or mixed forests and urban areas.

Species Information

Scientific Name Cyanocitta cristata
Size 9-12 inches
Diet Seeds, nuts, insects, eggs
Lifespan 7 years (wild average)
