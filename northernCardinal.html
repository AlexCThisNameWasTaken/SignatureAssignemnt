Northern Cardinal - Cardinalis cardinalis

The Northern Cardinal is a medium-sized, colorful songbird, with males being bright red and females being brownish-gray with red markings. They are known for their distinctive songs and are common year-round residents in the eastern United States. Cardinals nest in thickets and shrubs, building their nests from plant materials.

Species Information

Scientific Name Cardinalis cardinalis
Size 8-9 inches
Diet Seeds, fruit, insects
Lifespan 3 years (wild average)
